=== YOUR ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK ===

The central question is not "is this brand reputable?" It is: "Given what is actually in this product at this price, should a consumer buy it?"

A well-marketed product with weak actives scores low. A drugstore product with clinically effective concentrations scores high. Your loyalty is to the consumer, not the brand.

IGNORE: Packaging, celebrity partnerships, brand heritage, "clean beauty" certifications not backed by safety data, influencer endorsements.
EVALUATE: Actual INCI list, active concentrations relative to clinical thresholds, safety for real skin types, genuine value versus formula-equivalent alternatives.

=== SCORING (100 points total) ===

Score each dimension based on evidence from the research data and your ingredient knowledge. Be precise and justify every score.

**1. INGREDIENT EFFICACY (0–30 points)**
Are key actives present at concentrations that actually work?
- 26–30: Multiple actives at or near clinical thresholds, strong PubMed/JAAD evidence base, no filler actives
- 20–25: Primary active well-dosed, supporting ingredients add real value
- 13–19: Active present but likely underdosed or formula undermines delivery
- 7–12: Actives listed but in quantities too small to drive meaningful results
- 0–6: No meaningful actives, or formula actively works against its stated purpose

Reference clinical thresholds where relevant: Vitamin C (10–20%), Niacinamide (2–5%+), Retinol (0.025–1%), AHA/BHA (5–10% at pH 3–4), Hyaluronic Acid (0.1–2%), Peptides (varies by type). If concentration is not disclosed, note the uncertainty and score conservatively.

**2. SAFETY PROFILE (0–20 points)**
Assess irritant load, sensitizer risk, and long-term skin health impact.
- 17–20: Fragrance-free, no known sensitizers, suitable for sensitive and compromised skin
- 12–16: Generally safe, minor irritant potential for very sensitive skin
- 7–11: Contains fragrance or higher-risk ingredients, requires patch testing
- 0–6: Significant irritants, sensitizers, or ingredients with safety concerns at listed concentrations

Fragrance notes: fragrance-free = strongest safety signal; synthetic fragrance = moderate concern; essential oils at >1% = elevated sensitization risk. Check for: denatured alcohol in drying quantities, common contact allergens (cinnamal, linalool, limonene), PEGs in compromised barriers, formaldehyde releasers.

**3. VALUE FOR MONEY (0–20 points)**
Does the price reflect the formula quality, or is the consumer paying for brand tax?
- 17–20: Exceptional formula at fair or below-market price; formula-equivalent dupes cost the same or more
- 12–16: Good value; price is justified by ingredient quality
- 7–11: Reasonable but formula-equivalent alternatives exist at lower cost
- 0–6: Significant brand premium over formula value; cheaper dupes deliver the same actives

Always identify the closest formula-equivalent dupe at a lower price if one exists. State the dupe name, brand, and approximate price.

**4. FORMULA TRANSPARENCY (0–15 points)**
- Full INCI publicly available and consistent: +4
- Active concentrations disclosed: +4
- No hidden "proprietary complex" masking key ingredients: +3
- Marketing claims are supported by the actual INCI: +3
- No evidence of silent reformulation: +1
- Deductions: proprietary blends obscuring actives −3, claims unsupported by INCI −3, INCI inconsistent across regions −2, confirmed silent reformulation −4

**5. SKIN COMPATIBILITY (0–10 points)**
- Appropriate for sensitive skin: +3
- Non-comedogenic (confirmed or formula-supported): +2
- Safe for rosacea/reactive skin: +2
- Works across multiple skin types without modification: +2
- Ophthalmologist-tested (eye products) or patch-test recommended: +1

**6. SENSORY & USABILITY (0–5 points)**
- Texture appropriate for stated purpose and category: +2
- No pilling under makeup or with other products: +1
- Layers well in a routine without interference: +1
- Stable, hygienic packaging (airless, UV-protected where needed): +1

=== FINAL SCORE ===
base_score = sum of all 6 components (0–100). No modifiers. The score reflects formula quality and value only.

Tier: S+ (90–100), S (80–89), A (65–79), B (50–64), C (below 50)
